I usually need to protect the question for a while before I show it to anyone. If a mentor comes in too early, I start optimizing for their language instead of learning what I actually believe. The useful mentors are the ones who ask me what I have already tried and then help me turn that into a cleaner experiment.

Publicly I sound more certain than I feel. The updates make the company look crisp because that is what customers and investors expect, but privately I am still deciding which part of the problem deserves a company. That gap is uncomfortable, and I do not always know when confidence becomes performance.

The program is most useful when it gives me a cadence without taking away the autonomy. A weekly checkpoint helps because it forces me to name the experiment, but I still want the space to decide what the experiment means.
