Ambiguity does not bother me at the beginning. I like staying with the messy version of the idea because it keeps more possibilities alive. The risk is that I can defend exploration for too long and call it strategy when I am avoiding a market test.

I am careful with mentor feedback because a confident person can collapse the idea too fast. The advice I trust starts with context. If someone understands why I am keeping the question open, then I can hear the push to make it testable.

The public story is much sharper than the private reality. Online I describe a clear founder identity because people respond to clarity. In the actual work, I am still comparing three versions of the customer and trying to learn which one pulls me forward.
