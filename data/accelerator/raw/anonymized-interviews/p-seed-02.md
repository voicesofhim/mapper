I can execute quickly once the sequence is clear, but before that I keep making the plan better instead of testing it. The biggest shift was having a weekly milestone that told me exactly what customer discovery meant this week, not in theory.

Mentor advice works for me when it becomes a structure. A broad suggestion like talk to more buyers does not change my behavior. A specific sprint with five outreach messages, two interviews, and a Friday review makes me move.

I do worry that structure can hide whether I actually understand the customer. I can complete the tasks and still avoid the hard question. The program helps when the template includes a reflection step, because then execution and learning stay connected.
